# Black Hole in One — Playtest Notes

**Concept:** Turn-based flick golf across pocket planetary systems — drag anywhere and release to fling a comet; planets bend every shot AND act as fairways (land on a surface, tee off again from there); the cup is a black hole with a capture spiral. Infinite procedural holes, par tracking, running total vs par.

**Originality note (post-Flow-Glider rule):** Desert Golfing / Angry Birds were cited in the design source (vault §A2) for the *turn-based flick* feel only. Nothing else is lifted: radial-gravity planet-hopping, the black-hole cup, slingshot celebration, and repulsor pulsars (hole 6+) are this game's own hooks — there's no terrain scroll, no destruction, no bird cannon.

**How it pauses (Q2=B):** pause-proof by construction — between flicks the game is fully at rest, so interruption costs nothing. Mid-flight (~2 s) the sim auto-pauses on tab switch / window blur and resumes cleanly.

## What this playtest should evaluate

- **Does bending shots around gravity feel skillful rather than random?** Is the short trajectory preview (~half a second) the right length — enough to learn the physics, not so much it plays itself?
- **Is planet-hopping real?** Do you ever land on a planet *on purpose* to set up the next flick, or is every hole brute-forced with direct shots? If hopping goes unused, the hook needs strengthening (stickier landings, bigger blockers, longer holes).
- **Session shape:** does infinite holes + running total vs par give "one more hole", or does it want 9-hole rounds with a scorecard and a best-round record?
